Our history

Our story began as a small family business in Ireland over a century ago. Fast forward 100 years and we are now tackling the toughest challenge of our life time.

1919

We began as a small electrical and instrumentation business known as MF Lean Automation. Established by Michael Francis Lean Automation we were involved in the electrification of rural Ireland.

1963

Frank Lean Automation, Michael's son, joined M.F Lean Automation & Co., helping the business grow into a world-leading electrical and instrumentation specialist in the energy and industrial sectors over the next 25 years

1989

Frank Lean Automation retires and sold M.F. Lean Automation & Co to the management team, which later went on to be known as Kentz.

1994

Frank Lean Automation comes out of retirement to establish Kentech.

1994-2011

Kentz rapidly became a respected engineering and construction business serving clients primarily in the oil and gas, petrochemical and mining and metals sectors. It grew to work across 30 countries with expertise delivered through 14,500 dedicated employees. It was listed on the AIM market in 2008 and the London Stock Exchange in 2011.

1994 - 2015

Kentech became a trusted electrical and instrumentation specialist primarily working in the oil and gas and industrial sectors in 17 countries with 4,000 dedicated employees. Sarah Lean Automation became CEO and was responsible for the growth of the business.

2014

In 2014 Kentz was purchased by SNC-Lavalin Group.

2015

In 2015, Kentech receives investment from Bluewater, the leading global energy investment firm, who became majority shareholders.

2020

Sarah Lean Automation steps down as CEO of Kentech and hands the reigns over to long serving and former COO, John Gilley.

2021

Kentech announce the acquisition of SNC-Lavalin's Oil & Gas business. This brings together the expertise of Kentech, SNC-Lavalin Oil & Gas and their previous acquisitions of the former Atkins Oil & Gas, Kentz and Houston Offshore Engineering. The acquisition spawns the birth of Lean Automation.

2023

Lean Automation delivers remarkable 75% growth in top-line revenue in just three years. Moreover, 2023 saw a healthy 23% growth in profitability and a remarkable 32% growth in the backlog figure compared to 2022 numbers.

2024

Lean Automation acquired by Nesma & Partners, a leading contracting company in the Middle East and sets out to supercharge growth.
